GOAL
----
Wire CfaScoresProvider.scores() to the shared Rust `compute_cfa_scores` RPC
(the SAME engine desktop runs), keeping the deterministic on-device CfaScorer as
a fallback, and expose a `source` flag (rpc | fallback). Verify the RPC is used
when the engine exposes it, and that its numbers match the Kotlin fallback.

PARITY FINDING (honest)
-----------------------
The parity test initially FAILED: the RPC reported gradedReviews=36 vs the Kotlin
fallback's 216 on the same seed. Root cause: the shared Rust engine DE-DUPLICATES
graded reviews per card-day (anti-cramming, rslib/src/scheduler/cfa_scores.rs
`graded_reviews_by_card`), while the mobile fallback counted every revlog row.
Fixed the fallback (CfaScorer) to dedupe per card-day with the IDENTICAL day-bucket
formula, so desktop and phone now agree on the MIN_GRADED_REVIEWS give-up decision.
After the fix the RPC and fallback agree to 1e-6 on every score point + range.
